Doda
Sculpture
Concept
"Doda" is a sculpture that represents a group of riders involved in the Kokpar game. It embodies the themes of competition, resilience, and the connection between humans and horses in the context of ancient traditions. The form merges human strength with the dynamic movement of the game, reflecting both its historical roots and modern significance.
Processes & Tools
Carved from poplar wood using traditional tools, "Doda" captures the energy of the Kokpar game. The piece was created during the years 1994-1997 and reflects a period of intense cultural exploration. The artist worked meticulously to preserve the raw texture of the wood, enhancing its natural grains and knots to evoke the strength and movement of the riders.
Technical Details
Material: Poplar wood
Height: 50 cm
Tools: Chisels, rasps, sandpaper
Finish: Natural oil, matte
Year: 1994-1997
